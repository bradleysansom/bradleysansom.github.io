Found alphabet and letterspace projects
Evaluation
When I was told that I had to write an evaluation of this unit, I'll admit to being a bit worried. I'd not really been enjoying the project that much, I thought my work wasn't really up to standard and I'd not put enough effort into it. However, it's only in the process of writing everything up into these pages, collecting together all the work I've done, that I've reconsidered it.
I've actually really liked it. I was a bit worried that, given I'm not a very good photographer, or a very good illustrator, that I would struggle with the two briefs. And I did, for a while. However, I've taken the briefs in a direction that I'm really proud of.
With regard to research, the Type Player series of books has been a great help. I much prefer looking through a curated selection of works, flicking from page to page, rather than endlessly scrolling through Pinterest or Google Images. That's not to mean that I've not taken my research in other directions; I've looked at quite a wide range of sources, illustrations, photography et cetera. Quite a bit of the research was also personal; looking through an old hard drive to find all the stuff I made in Microsoft Office as a young kid, I found that I've been (subconsciosuly) designing things for years.
When the brief for the second project put such an emphasis on having a story and a personal connection, I panicked a bit. I always do with stuff like that, I just always suppose I've got nothing especially revolutionary or groundbreaking to say. But realising the journey I've been on for years with regard to my attitude to art, the 'u-turn', I've been able to explore my life, and my mind, in quite a deep way through design.
I happened upon my idea for the letterspace project quite quickly in comparison to others. I remember being astounded at the first seminar when I had a couple of sketches and a little list, and others had done a huge moodboard and had 50 ideas. But I shouldn't have worried, as the development came a bit later but got quite deep and personal.
My final pieces don't follow the briefs to the letter; I had an urge to make MORE. Therefore I've done two interactive posters rather than just the one, and I've done eight variations of the letter. Perhaps I ought to learn to sift this down a bit, be a bit more critical of my ideas.
For the found alphabet project, I'm reasonably happy with my final outcomes. If I was doing it again or had more time, I'd make the interactive digital version a bit nicer. Currently it's very stark and simple, I could use the opportunity of a digital space to play about with motion and animation a bit more.
For the letterspace project, I wanted to produce something a bit more animated too. Perhaps a video version, showing the u-turn in a more dynamic way. I also had an idea to make a triptych of the U at different stages of its completion, but decided just to concentrate on the one layout for the final piece.
If I was doing the found alphabet project again, I think I would've walked around with a list of letters that I could cross off. I spent a lot of time taking duplicate pictures whilst not getting some for others until quite late.
If I was doing the letterspace project again, I would refine the figure itself. I think I should've used more of a regular, curved U instead of the freehand right half that I just made with the pen tool.
Despite these few regrets, on the whole, I'm pleased with the work I've done in this unit. I've really enjoyed it.
