The Bradford Pit Project commemorates the colliery that used to stand on the site now occupied by Sportcity and the Etihad Stadium.

Schools and educational activities

Learn with us

Discover educational activities that will ignite historical exploration of the Bradford Colliery

The Bradford Pit Project aims to revive the memory of the colliery and educate and engage a new audience about its history and its legacy, much of which is still relevant today. Why? We understand that it is important to acknowledge the heritage that the site offers and understand its significance as it has consequently impacted the development and the lives of those in the area. This Schools and Educational Activities page can therefore be used as a tool to achieve our objective whilst also sharing information about the colliery in a fun and engaging way. We have also had great success in our previous in-person activities which you can see below, involving people of all ages, cultures and backgrounds welcoming public input in order to integrate communities through heritage.

Guidance for teachers

In this space you will find a range of activities you can use as a class, in small groups or as individuals to enable students to learn about the project, the colliery, and the stories we have highlighted. We suggest allowing time for your students to explore this page where they will find unique, interactive, and engaging material that will stimulate their comprehensive skills along with their creativity and imagination. Whilst some of the material is targeted towards Secondary school students, there is a range of activities for all levels so that all students will be able to learn, understand, and have fun whilst exploring history. To help, we have recommended the appropriate age range for each activity so you as the teacher can guide your students as they learn.

Guidance for students

This webpage is full of creative activities that will introduce you to the history of the Bradford coal mine. The activities below will help you to learn about the proud past of the site, its people and its surroundings and will also allow you to use your imagination as you begin important discussions about the site. We have pulled together a range of activities for you to do including worksheets and creative writing, and have highlighted our Audio Library so you can listen to some of the miners' real stories - so get exploring!

Activities

Print work

This activity will give students the ability to create their own material commemorating the colliery and the miners. Using materials like charcoal, paint and fabric, see our featured story from 31 March 2015 where ex-miner Brian Grayson preserved the Bradford branch's National Union of Miners banner and create your own banner design! This activity can be done whilst verbally sharing digestible information about what our project is, why the project was started, and what the colliery was - all available on our About page. This activity is recommended for lower Primary school students and above.

Explore our interactive map

Able to explore our fully interactive map as a class, small group or individually, students will gain a great understanding of just how big the colliery was. There are also many stories to explore, and plenty of opportunities to begin interesting discussions. This activity is recommended for mid-high Primary school students and above.

Watch and listen to our oral history collection including our animated videos

Our Audio Library features real-life stories told by those who worked at the pit along with recordings from other people such as the miners' wives. We have also animated a few of these stories! This activity is recommended for mid-high Primary school students and above.

Try your hand at creative writing

Use the activities on this webpage as a tool to help you understand the lives of people affected by the colliery. Use this to help create a short story pretending you are a minor, a miner's family member, or whoever else you can think of. Perhaps you could even create a storyboard to tell your story where you can draw images to bring your imagination to life!

Use our audio library to discuss important topics

Our collection of oral histories gives great information shared by people with first-hand experience of the pit and told in their voices. Use this collection to discuss what the impact the colliery's closure in 1968 had on a) the community b) families c) minors themselves, and d) occupational choice. Think about the camaraderie and relationships developed inside and outside the mine! This activity is recommended for Secondary school students and above.

Explore the stories available on our website to discuss the pit's attraction

Using our interactive map, focus on the Cemetery and acknowledge the amount of people associated with the pit and read the stories we have highlighted. Also explore our Accidents and Incidents page. Use these stories to help you discuss as a class, in small groups or individually why you think that despite the Pitt being a dangerous and unpredictable environment, why so many people chose to work there? This activity is recommended for Secondary school students and above.
